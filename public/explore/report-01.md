## Style Fingerprint

### Typography
- **Script / calligraphy** for names and personal touches — warm, human, not corporate
- **Classical serif** for body text and formal copy — dignified, editorial
- **Block print / letterpress** aesthetic — intentional roughness, texture, craft
- Mix of register: formal (the chandelier engraving) and casual (hand-painted oysters)

### Illustration & Motifs
- Botanical and regional: oysters, candelabras, pagoda lanterns, turtles, floral engraving
- Hand-illustrated, not vector-clean — pen weight varies, edges aren't perfect
- Vintage postage stamps as decorative collage elements — suggests a love of "ephemera" and found objects

### Layout
- **Collage / flatlay composition** — layered, overlapping, not on a grid
- Density is high but feels curated, not chaotic — each element earns its place
- **Maximalist in spirit, muted in execution** — many elements, low saturation = it breathes

### Mood words
`Warm` · `Editorial` · `Artisanal` · `Curated` · `Vintage` · `Rooted` · `Tactile` · `Cultured`

## What This Tells Me About Elena's Taste

1. **She prefers warmth over coolness.** The cool blue is present but it's the accent, not the lead. Her default aesthetic runs warm.

2. **She likes muted, earthy palettes.** None of these colors are bright or digital. They feel like they were mixed by hand — slightly dusty, slightly aged.

3. **She's drawn to craft and intentionality.** Letterpress, wax seals, hand calligraphy — she responds to things that have a "made by a person" quality, not templated.

4. **She can hold multiple colors at once.** This isn't a two-color system — it's a 5-color palette that coheres because everything is muted. She's comfortable with richness.

5. **Editorial over minimal.** She does not gravitate toward pure Swiss minimalism. She wants substance, layering, and visual interest — but edited, not cluttered.

6. **A sense of place matters.** The New Orleans reference (Brennan's, oysters, the South) isn't accidental. She likes things that feel specific and located, not generic.

## Translation to Web Portfolio

The direct aesthetic won't transfer 1:1 — a letterpress wedding suite is not a PM portfolio. But the underlying taste can:

| What she likes | Web equivalent |
|---|---|
| Dusty salmon as the dominant warm | Primary brand color in that register |
| Sage green as a grounding accent | Secondary / highlight color |
| Cream paper stock | Warm off-white background (not pure `#fff`) |
| Terracotta pop | CTA buttons, highlights, hover states |
| Serif + script pairing | Serif display font for headings, clean sans for body |
| Craft texture | Subtle grain, slight shadows — not flat plastic UI |
| Layered editorial composition | Card components with depth, not flat tiles |
